Street
Art RTW

Documenting Inspired Walls Around The World.

In early 2015, after living in New York City for over three years, a window of opportunity came along for me to step outside the life I had made and do something I had never done before: travel (quite literally) around the world.

Three months later, after recruiting a travel buddy, relocating, planning, and saving, the two of us met in my friend’s hometown of Austin, TX, TX, where we prepared to set off into the unknown. I began taking photos of the public art, murals, and graffiti all over the city, and, having always been drawn to street art, it seemed a natural subject to continue documenting abroad.

What follows is a slightly obsessive (though hardly definitive) collection of street art that I stumbled upon in sixteen different countries across Europe, Asia, Oceania, and North America as I circled the globe, with additions from a few other short travels closer to home.

Enjoy!

Hi! I’m JT.

Before traveling about, I was a graphic designer at Grooveshark in New York City. I enjoyed hot tea on chilly mornings, listening to albums in their entirety, wandering side streets, training my cats to high five, Brian Eno’s ‘77 Million Paintings’, and learning new things.

Post-travel, I am a freelance designer. I enjoy all the same things, plus some new ones like riding motorcycles, wearing elephant pants, hygge, fika, building this site, planning the next adventure, and continuing to train my cats to high five.

In short… ‘Same same, but different.’
